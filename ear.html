"The Two Fridas" (1939):

In this powerful painting, Kahlo depicts two versions of herself sitting side by side, connected by intertwined blood vessels. One Frida wears a European-style dress, while the other wears traditional Tehuana attire. The painting explores Kahlo's dual heritage and identity, as well as her struggles with duality and self-acceptance. It reflects her embrace of Mexican culture and indigenous traditions, as well as her defiance of societal norms and expectations.
