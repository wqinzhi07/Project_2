"The Broken Column" (1944)

This haunting self-portrait shows Kahlo standing in front of a broken column, with her body split open to reveal a crumbling column inside. Metal nails pierce her skin, symbolizing the physical pain she experienced as a result of her injuries from a bus accident. The painting conveys Kahlo's resilience and strength in the face of adversity, as well as her exploration of pain, suffering, and healing.
