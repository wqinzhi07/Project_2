Title: The Broken Column

Artist: Frida Kahlo (1907-1954)

Year: 1944

Medium: Oil on canvas

Description: This is a haunting self-portrait that portrays Frida Kahlo standing in front of a shattered column, her body split open to reveal a crumbling column inside. Metal nails pierce her skin, symbolizing the physical pain she endured following a severe bus accident. Through this powerful imagery, Kahlo conveys not only the physical anguish she experienced but also her resilience and strength in the face of adversity. The painting serves as a poignant exploration of pain, suffering, and healing, inviting viewers to contemplate the profound emotional and physical struggles Kahlo confronted throughout her life.
